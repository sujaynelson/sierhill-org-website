# Mechanobiology Research Analysis

## Your Mechanobiology Papers

All from the Sokol Lab, Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai. Key authors: Velayudhan SS, Chu CW, Itoh K, Alfandari D, Sokol SY.

### 1. Mechanical cues polarize ADIP protein complexes to control vertebrate morphogenesis and wound healing
- **Journal:** Current Biology (2025)
- **DOI:** [10.1016/j.cub.2025.05.069](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cub.2025.05.069)
- **Key finding:** ADIP puncta relocate in response to pulling forces from apically constricting cells. ADIP forms a mechanosensitive complex with PCP protein Diversin, distinct from known core PCP complexes, and is required for wound repair. Mechanically controlled planar polarization of the ADIP-Diversin complex guides cell behaviors in normal morphogenesis and wound healing.

### 2. Mechanosensitive localization of Diversin highlights its function in vertebrate morphogenesis and planar cell polarity
- **Journal:** Biology Open (2025)
- **DOI:** [10.1242/bio.062128](https://doi.org/10.1242/bio.062128)
- **Key finding:** Diversin acquires planar polarity in the neuroectoderm in a stage- and position-specific manner. Diversin puncta redistribute toward pulling forces from cells with constricting apical domains. Depletion of Diversin inhibits apical domain size and neural tube closure and disrupts polarized localization of endogenous Vangl2.

### 3. Planar polarization of endogenous ADIP during Xenopus neurulation
- **Journal:** Biology Open (2026)
- **DOI:** [10.1242/bio.062452](https://doi.org/10.1242/bio.062452)
- **Key finding:** Endogenous ADIP is present in early ectoderm as randomly distributed puncta that reorganize and polarize during epithelial wound repair. ADIP becomes enriched and planar polarized in the anterior neural plate toward the midline. ADIP polarization is attenuated by depletion of Diversin/Ankrd6 and eliminated by pharmacological disruption of microtubules, F-actin, and nonmuscle myosin II.

### 4. Linking Planar Cell Polarity to Mechanotransduction During Morphogenesis (Review)
- **Journal:** BioEssays (2026)
- **DOI:** [10.1002/bies.70107](https://doi.org/10.1002/bies.70107)
- **Authors:** Egorova KS, Sokol SY
- **Key finding:** Proposes a positive regulatory loop connecting PCP to mechanotransduction. A PCP axis can form in response to intrinsic or extrinsic mechanical stresses, and PCP signaling in turn controls physical force production in the tissue. This mechanochemical feedback is proposed as a fundamental mechanism ensuring proper tissue shape during morphogenesis.

## Future Research Directions

### 1. Identify the molecular mechanism of ADIP mechanosensing
- **Gap:** The papers show ADIP *responds* to mechanical forces, but the mechanism is unknown. Does ADIP directly sense tension (like a catch bond), or respond indirectly via cytoskeletal remodeling?
- **Approach:** Domain truncation/mutagenesis of ADIP, FRET-based tension sensors, reconstitution with purified proteins on stretched membranes.
- **Impact:** High — this is the central unanswered question from the existing work.

### 2. ADIP-Diversin in other force-dependent morphogenetic contexts
- **Gap:** The complex has been shown in neurulation and wound healing. It likely operates in convergent extension, heart tube formation, and kidney tubulogenesis — all PCP-dependent, force-driven processes.
- **Approach:** Examine ADIP/Diversin localization during Xenopus convergent extension, or in mouse/zebrafish models. Relevant preprints include mechanical pacemakers in nephron formation and CFL1 in spinal zippering.
- **Impact:** High — generalizes the discovery beyond neurulation.

### 3. ADIP/Diversin variants in human neural tube defects (NTDs)
- **Gap:** PCP mutations (Vangl2, Dvl) cause NTDs in humans. ADIP/Diversin variants have not been screened in NTD patient cohorts.
- **Approach:** Mine exome/genome datasets (gnomAD, MGRB) for ADIP/SSX2IP and ANKRD6 variants in NTD patients. Test candidate variants in Xenopus.
- **Impact:** High — translational relevance; could identify new genetic risk factors for spina bifida/anencephaly.

### 4. Quantitative modeling of the mechanochemical feedback loop
- **Gap:** The Egorova & Sokol review proposes PCP-mechanotransduction feedback but there is no quantitative model yet. Collaborator Ala Trusina (University of Copenhagen) is already on the author list.
- **Approach:** Build a computational model coupling PCP protein dynamics with tissue-level mechanical forces, then validate experimentally.
- **Impact:** Medium-high — transforms a qualitative hypothesis into a predictive framework.

### 5. Cross-talk with established mechanosensors (YAP/TAZ, Piezo)
- **Gap:** YAP/TAZ and Piezo channels are well-established mechanotransducers. Whether and how the ADIP-Diversin system cross-talks with these pathways is unknown.
- **Approach:** Epistasis experiments — does ADIP polarization require Piezo channel activity? Does ADIP modulate YAP nuclear localization?
- **Impact:** Medium — places the novel complex within the broader mechanosensing field.

### 6. ADIP in adult epithelial wound healing (therapeutic angle)
- **Gap:** ADIP-Diversin is required for embryonic wound repair. Adult wound healing is a major clinical field where PCP's role is underexplored.
- **Approach:** Test in adult Xenopus skin or mammalian cell monolayer wound assays. Screen for small molecules that enhance ADIP polarization.
- **Impact:** Medium — potential translational application.
